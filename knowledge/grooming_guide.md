# Grooming Schedules and Tips

Grooming isn't just cosmetic — it's how you check for skin issues, lumps, parasites, and ear/dental problems early.

## Brushing

- **Short-haired dogs** (Beagle, Boxer, Pit Bull, Pug): weekly, 5–10 minutes. A rubber curry brush works well.
- **Medium-haired dogs** (Golden Retriever, Labrador, Border Collie, German Shepherd): 2–3 times per week, 10–15 minutes. Slicker brush + undercoat rake during shedding seasons.
- **Long-haired dogs** (Yorkie, Shih Tzu, Maltese, Afghan Hound): daily, 15–20 minutes. Mats form quickly.
- **Double-coated breeds** (Husky, Samoyed, Pomeranian): 2–3 times per week normally, daily during spring/fall shedding ("blowing coat"). Never shave double-coated breeds.

## Bathing

- **Dogs**: every 4–8 weeks for most breeds. Over-bathing strips natural oils. Use dog-specific shampoo (human shampoo has the wrong pH).
- **Cats**: rarely need baths. Only if they get into something they can't self-clean.
- **Active or outdoor pets**: more frequent baths are fine if you're using a gentle shampoo.

## Nail trims

- **Cadence**: every 3–4 weeks for most pets.
- **Sign you waited too long**: nails clicking on hard floors, or nails curling.
- **Tip**: if your pet hates clippers, try a Dremel-style grinder. Trim small amounts frequently rather than a big trim once.
- **Time per session**: 5–10 minutes.

## Teeth

- **Brushing**: ideally daily, but 2–3 times per week is meaningful. Use pet-specific toothpaste (human toothpaste contains xylitol, which is toxic to dogs).
- **Dental chews**: VOHC-approved chews supplement but don't replace brushing.
- **Professional cleaning**: typically every 1–2 years, more often for small breeds prone to dental disease.

## Ear cleaning

- **Cadence**: monthly for most pets, weekly for floppy-eared breeds (Cocker Spaniel, Basset Hound, Poodle) and dogs that swim.
- **Signs of ear problems**: head shaking, scratching, brown/yellow discharge, foul smell. These warrant a vet visit, not just cleaning.

## Anal glands

- Most dogs express their glands naturally during bowel movements.
- Scooting on the floor, excessive licking, or a fishy odor can indicate impaction.
- Groomers and vets can express manually — usually every 4–8 weeks if your dog needs it.

## Professional grooming

- **Short-coated breeds**: usually optional.
- **Poodles, Doodles, Yorkies, Shih Tzus, Schnauzers**: every 6–8 weeks for full grooming (cut, bath, nails).
- **Costs**: $40–$120 depending on size, coat condition, and region.

## Time per grooming task

For scheduling purposes, here are typical durations:
- Quick brush-out: 5–10 minutes
- Thorough brush + de-shed: 20–30 minutes
- Bath at home: 30–45 minutes (small dog), 45–60 minutes (large dog)
- Nail trim: 5–10 minutes
- Teeth brushing: 2–3 minutes
- Ear cleaning: 5 minutes
