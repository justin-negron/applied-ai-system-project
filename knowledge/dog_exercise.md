# Dog Exercise Guidelines

Daily exercise needs vary widely by breed, age, and health status. These are general guidelines — always defer to your vet for individualized advice.

## By breed group

- **Working and sporting breeds** (Golden Retriever, Labrador, Border Collie, German Shepherd, Husky, Australian Shepherd): 60–120 minutes of moderate-to-vigorous activity daily. Splitting into a morning and evening session works well. These breeds become destructive when under-exercised.
- **Terriers** (Jack Russell, Bull Terrier, Yorkshire Terrier): 45–60 minutes daily. High energy in short bursts.
- **Hounds** (Beagle, Greyhound, Basset Hound): 30–60 minutes. Some hounds (Greyhounds) actually have low energy needs despite their athletic build — short sprints over long jogs.
- **Toy breeds** (Chihuahua, Pomeranian, Maltese): 20–30 minutes daily. Indoor play counts.
- **Brachycephalic breeds** (Pug, Bulldog, French Bulldog, Boxer): 15–30 minutes only, in cool weather. They overheat fast and have breathing limitations.
- **Giant breeds** (Great Dane, Saint Bernard, Mastiff): 30–45 minutes of low-impact exercise. Avoid high-impact activity until joints are mature (~18–24 months).

## By age

- **Puppies** (under 12 months): the "5-minute rule" — 5 minutes of structured exercise per month of age, twice daily. A 4-month-old puppy: ~20 minutes twice daily.
- **Adult dogs** (1–7 years): full breed-typical activity.
- **Senior dogs** (7+, varies by breed): shorter, more frequent walks. Watch for stiffness or reluctance.

## Warm weather caution

In temperatures above 80°F (27°C), shorten walks and avoid pavement (test with the back of your hand for 5 seconds — if it's too hot for you, it's too hot for paws). Brachycephalic breeds should not exercise above 75°F.

## Splitting walks

Two shorter walks usually beat one long walk for behavior and digestion. A typical schedule: 20–30 minute morning walk, 20–30 minute evening walk, plus play sessions and bathroom breaks throughout the day.
