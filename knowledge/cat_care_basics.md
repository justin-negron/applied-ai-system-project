# Cat Care Basics

Cats hide illness well, so consistent daily routines matter — they're how you'll notice when something is off.

## Feeding

- **Adult cats** (1–7 years): two meals per day is standard. Free-feeding dry food works for some cats but contributes to obesity. Wet food once or twice daily is generally better for hydration.
- **Kittens** (under 12 months): three to four small meals per day until ~6 months, then transition to twice daily.
- **Senior cats** (7+): may need smaller, more frequent meals. Watch for weight loss — it's often the first sign of kidney or thyroid issues.
- **Fresh water** should be available at all times. Many cats prefer running water (fountains).

## Litter box

- Scoop daily. Cats are particular and may stop using a dirty box.
- Rule of thumb: one box per cat, plus one extra. Two cats = three boxes.
- Full litter change every 1–2 weeks for clay litter; less often for clumping litter (just refill).

## Play and enrichment

- Indoor cats need 15–30 minutes of active play daily, ideally split into two sessions (morning and evening). Wand toys, laser pointers, and feather teasers work well.
- Rotate toys every few days to prevent boredom.
- Provide vertical space — cats love high perches.

## Grooming

- **Short-haired cats**: brush weekly.
- **Long-haired cats** (Persian, Maine Coon, Ragdoll, Siamese with long coat): brush 3–4 times per week to prevent mats.
- Most cats don't need baths unless they get into something. They self-groom effectively.
- Trim nails every 2–3 weeks.

## Warning signs to watch for

Get a vet appointment if you notice any of these for more than 24 hours:
- Not eating or drinking
- Hiding more than usual
- Litter box changes (straining, frequent trips, blood in urine)
- Vomiting more than once or twice
- Lethargy or limping

These are signs to discuss with a vet — they are not a substitute for professional veterinary care.
